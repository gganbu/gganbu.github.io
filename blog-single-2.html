How we came up with the title "Lifeline"

By Marc • 2 mins read
Scroll

The story of our game title

After creating and writing the story, the next step was to give our game a title. Since the theme of the story and the game focused on signs of depression, it was clear in which direction the title should lean. Our initial idea was "Are you okay?"â€”a direct question to our in-game character, Alex, who is struggling with depression. While emotionally impactful and meaningful, we were dissatisfied with the title because it wasn't a single word and might reveal too much about the game's content.

Later, we settled on our current and final title, "Lifeline." It's subtle and carries an indirect meaning related to depression. Our concept was that the game could be interpreted as a thin line that keeps Alex alive. This line represents the friend, the role the player assumes in the game to help Alex feel better and seek professional assistance to overcome depression. Compared to our initial title, "Lifeline" is much more fitting for our game and encapsulates its overall meaning effectively.
