About Lifeline

Lifeline

An interactive 2D game about the signs of depression

The game revolves around the player's interaction with a character named Alex. Through various challenges and choices, the player learns more about Alex's life, personality, and struggles. The narrative unfolds based on the player's decisions, influencing the relationship between the player and Alex. The game incorporates elements such as solving puzzles, receiving messages, and making choices that impact the overall storyline. Ultimately, the goal is to engage the player emotionally and create a unique and personalized gaming experience.

Story & Showcase

“
In the opening acts, Alex and the player, already acquainted, engage in a casual chat within a digital realm. Alex, a struggling artist, hints at underlying challenges, setting the stage for a deeper connection. As their conversation progresses, the player navigates the complexities of Alex's emotions and the creative struggles. The foundation of trust is established, leading to a shared exploration of the unknown facets of Alex's life. This lays the groundwork for a journey that transcends the boundaries of a typical chatroom, delving into the intricate tapestry of human emotions and experiences.

Chapter 1-2

"Reunion"
“
As the connection between Alex and the player deepens, Act 3 explores the challenges of self-doubt and the importance of mutual support. Alex opens up about inner struggles, and the player offers understanding and encouragement. In Act 4, the narrative takes a creative turn as Alex introduces a digital art project. The player engages in mini-games related to the story, fostering a dynamic interaction. This act serves as a bridge, connecting emotional exploration with creative expression, laying the groundwork for the evolving relationship.

Chapter 3-4

"Building Bridges"
“
As the story unfolds, Alex confronts overwhelming thoughts and a sense of purposelessness. The player witnesses a pivotal moment as Alex contemplates seeking professional help. Tensions rise, and the narrative takes a serious turn. The player offers support and encouragement, culminating in Alex's decision to pursue therapy. The acts explore themes of inner struggle, resilience, and the transformative power of seeking help. The evolving dynamics between Alex and the player set the stage for a profound and uncertain resolution, emphasizing the importance of facing one's shadows for personal growth.

Chapter 5-6

"Facing the Truth"

Game Features

Character
Interaction

The player engages with the character Alex, uncovering details about their life, interests, and challenges.

Choice-Based
Narrative

The storyline is shaped by the player's decisions, affecting the relationship dynamics between the player and Alex

Puzzle
Solving

Players must solve puzzles, such as piecing together images or finding important objects, to progress in the game.

Message
Reception

The player receives messages, emails, or other forms of communication from Alex, providing additional layers to the story.

Emotional
Engagement

The game aims to evoke emotions and create a personalized experience, fostering a strong connection between the player and the character.

Influence on
Story

Choices made by the player have consequences, influencing the overall narrative and potentially leading to different outcomes.
